## ğŸ” The Challenge

Higher education is navigating unprecedented change. At Suffolk University and the Sawyer Business School, like many institutions, we faced several interconnected challenges:

### The Evaluation Clarity Problem

For years, faculty performance reviews lacked clear, shared criteria. Associate Dean Tracey Riley articulated this challenge in her June 2025 communication:

> *"I've spoken openly about the challenges of this process, particularly the difficulty of evaluating without established, faculty-approved criteria."*

This ambiguity created:
- **Uncertainty** for faculty about what "meets expectations" actually means
- **Inconsistency** in how different departments and chairs evaluated similar work
- **Frustration** when ratings didn't align with faculty self-perceptions
- **Missed opportunities** for meaningful professional development conversations

### The SMART Goals Initiative

In response, SBS introduced a new SMART goals framework with a two-track evaluation system:

| Track | Purpose | Outcome |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **Baseline** | Meet expectations in Teaching, Scholarship, and Service | COLA eligibility |
| **Merit** | Achieve ambitious SMART goals beyond baseline | Rating of 4 or 5 |

But this raised new questions: *What exactly does "meets expectations" mean in each area? How do we ensure equity across departments? How do we honor diverse forms of faculty contribution?*

### ISOM's Response

Rather than wait for top-down answers, the **Information Systems & Operations Management (ISOM) Department** took initiative to develop a thoughtful, faculty-centered framework that:

- âœ… Defines clear baseline expectations
- âœ… Provides multiple pathways for scholarship
- âœ… Aligns with AACSB accreditation standards
- âœ… Maintains equity with other SBS departments
- âœ… Creates meaningful distinction between baseline and merit-worthy work

## ğŸ’¡ Our Approach

### Guiding Principles

We built this framework on six core principles:

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **AACSB-Aligned** | Criteria reflect AACSB standards for faculty qualification and intellectual contributions |
| **Role-Appropriate** | Expectations vary by appointment type (SA vs. PA qualification) |
| **Context-Sensitive** | Evaluation considers circumstances while maintaining consistent standards |
| **Multi-Year Perspective** | Scholarly productivity assessed over 2-3 year trajectory, not annual checklist |
| **Equity Across SBS** | Standards consistent with other departments (using Marketing's benchmark) |
| **Clear Distinction** | Baseline performance distinguishable from merit-worthy achievement |
