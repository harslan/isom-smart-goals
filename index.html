Understanding SMART Goals

What Makes a Goal "SMART"?

SMART goals provide a framework for setting clear, achievable objectives that can be meaningfully evaluated.

S
Specific
Clear and well-defined objectives, not vague aspirations
M
Measurable
Quantifiable outcomes that can be tracked and verified
A
Achievable
Realistic given available resources and constraints
R
Relevant
Aligned with department goals and institutional priorities
T
Time-bound
Clear deadline for completion (typically end of AY)

Scholarship Pathways

Three Paths to Meeting Expectations

ISOM recognizes multiple forms of intellectual contribution. Choose the pathway that fits your work.

A

Scholarly Academic (Research)

Traditional disciplinary research for SA-qualified faculty

Multi-Year Expectation
1-2 scholarly outputs over a 2-3 year period
Qualifying Outputs Include:
  • Conference presentations or workshop participation
  • Working papers or manuscripts in development
  • Publications (accepted, in press, or published)
  • Grant proposals submitted or ongoing
  • Research collaborations with tangible products
B

Scholarly Academic (Teaching Scholarship)

Scholarship of teaching and learning for SA-qualified faculty

Multi-Year Expectation
1-2 scholarly outputs in teaching scholarship over 2-3 years
Qualifying Outputs Include:
  • Presentations at teaching-focused conferences (AACSB, AIS)
  • Publications in teaching journals or pedagogical outlets
  • Educational grant proposals or funded projects
  • Curriculum innovation with systematic assessment
  • Publications in venues like AACSB Insights, DSJIE
Note: Teaching scholarship requires systematic inquiry and dissemination beyond simply excellent teaching.
C

Practice Academic

Professional engagement for PA-qualified faculty

Ongoing Expectation
~7-10 hours/week of discipline-relevant professional activity
Qualifying Activities Include:
  • Professional consulting or advisory work
  • Industry collaboration and applied projects
  • Professional certification maintenance
  • Active practice in professional domain
  • Executive education development
  • Professional association leadership

What Merit Looks Like

Sample goals that clearly exceed baseline expectations and warrant merit ratings.

4
Exceeds Expectations
"Publish 2 peer-reviewed articles and present at 2 national conferences by May 2026"

Why it works: Substantially exceeds the 1-2 output baseline, with multiple high-impact deliverables.

5
Far Exceeds Expectations
"Secure external grant funding ($50K+), publish in top-tier journal, and present at 2 national conferences by May 2026"

Why it works: Major external funding + top publication + multiple presentations = transformative achievement.

4
Exceeds Expectations
"Develop new experiential learning module across 3 courses with assessed student outcomes by May 2026"
