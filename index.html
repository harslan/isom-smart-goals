Guiding Principles

We built this framework on six principles that balance accountability with flexibility:

AACSB-Aligned

Criteria reflect AACSB standards for faculty qualification and intellectual contributions.

Role-Appropriate

Expectations vary by appointment type (SA vs. PA qualification).

Context-Sensitive

Evaluation considers individual circumstances while maintaining consistent standards.

Multi-Year Perspective

Scholarly productivity assessed over 2-3 year trajectory, not as an annual checklist.

Equity Across SBS

Standards consistent with other departments, using Marketing's benchmark.

Clear Distinction

Baseline performance is distinguishable from merit-worthy exceptional achievement.

Sample SMART Goals

What does merit-worthy performance look like? Here are examples that clearly exceed baseline expectations:

4
Exceeds Expectations
"Publish 2 peer-reviewed articles and present at 2 national conferences by May 2026"

Why it works: Substantially exceeds the 1-2 output baseline, with multiple high-impact deliverables.

5
Far Exceeds Expectations
"Secure external grant funding ($50K+), publish in top-tier journal, and present at 2 national conferences by May 2026"

Why it works: Major external funding + top publication + multiple presentations = transformative achievement.

4
Exceeds Expectations (Teaching Scholarship)
"Develop new experiential learning module across 3 courses with assessed student outcomes, present findings at AACSB conference by May 2026"

Why it works: Significant curriculum innovation with systematic assessment and national dissemination.

Key Takeaways for Faculty

Achievable Standards

Context Matters

Meeting baseline qualifies for COLA; SMART goals determine merit ratings (4 or 5).
